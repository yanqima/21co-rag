Internet of Things (IoT) Revolution

The Internet of Things refers to the network of physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, enabling them to collect and exchange data. IoT is transforming industries and daily life.

IoT Applications:
- Smart Homes: Thermostats, security systems, appliances
- Wearables: Fitness trackers, smartwatches, health monitors
- Industrial IoT: Predictive maintenance, supply chain optimization
- Smart Cities: Traffic management, waste management, energy efficiency
- Agriculture: Precision farming, crop monitoring, irrigation control

Key Technologies:
1. Sensors and Actuators
2. Connectivity Protocols (WiFi, Bluetooth, 5G)
3. Edge Computing
4. Cloud Platforms
5. Data Analytics

Security Considerations:
- Device authentication
- Data encryption
- Regular firmware updates
- Network segmentation

By 2025, it's estimated there will be over 75 billion IoT devices worldwide.
